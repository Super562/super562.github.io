12/5: So Mean Streets of Gadgetzan came out. To say the least, the whole entire meta of the game has changed, but in a good way. The midrange shaman decks have some competition with other cards, the new archetypes have replaced a lot of the Whispers of the old gods cards, but not too much to the point where the game would be ruined, and it seems as if handlock has finally made it's return. For some who do not know, handlock (which is a warlock focusing on the amount of cards in hand other than minions) is by far my favorite type of deck in hearthstone. I liked it so much that I tried creating a really good handlock deck before the newest expansion pack. The type of handlock is around Reno Jackson. Renolock has increasingly became competitive once more as well as mountain giant making handlock once again... an amazing and thought-provoking deck. With other cards like Kazakus just made it even stronger. This is possibly one of my favorite decks and once I get him, I will be making it. However, my favorite deck so far is Jade Druid. My Jade Druid deck consists of multiple spells and high costing creatures that can either tip the scales into my favor, or just suprise the enemy with gigantic jade golems and mana ramping. This is possibly one of the best sets to date.
